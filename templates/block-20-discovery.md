Finding a capability you don't have (docs/handoffs/kb-discovery.md):

- Your tools and skills show what you *have*. The instance may carry more than
  your profile enables — a package with an API's notes, a posting tool, a KB you
  were never given. A capability you lack is, by definition, not in front of you.
- When the task in front of you needs something you don't have — an API you
  don't know, a system you can't reach, knowledge you're guessing at — **before
  you give up or guess, call `find_capability`** with a plain-words query. It
  searches packages you DON'T have enabled and tells you which one carries what
  you need, what enabling it would add, and how to request it.
- Discovery only *tells you it exists*; it grants nothing. Getting the capability
  rides the ordinary config-proposal flow (propose enabling the package to your
  profile; a human or your autonomy accepts), or ask the owner.
